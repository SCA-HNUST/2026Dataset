Abstract

Far Field Electromagnetic Side-Channel Attacks (FEM-SCAs), also known as screaming channel attacks, represent a growing threat to RF-enabled IoT devices, as they can be conducted remotely over the air. While prior research has demonstrated various FEM-SCA techniques, most studies remain limited to specific case studies.

In this paper, we bridge that gap through a practical and extensive FEM-SCA evaluation. We first construct and publicly release a novel dataset, the Screaming Channel 2026 dataset (SC2026), comprising far-field EM traces captured from multiple crypto (AES, SM4, Kyber) across distances (1 - 30 m) and through various barriers (brick, metal, plastic). Afterwards, we perform a detailed leakage assessment and experimentally evaluate more than 10 attack methods, ranging from classical CPA to modern deep learning approaches including Transformer-based models, establishing a systematic benchmark for future research.

The SC2026 Dataset

SC2026 is designed to simulate real-world scenarios for IoT security evaluation. It supports both profiling (template building) and attack evaluation under challenging conditions.

Systematic Benchmark

We evaluated over 10 attack methods, including Switch-T Transformer, DAR-NN, and classical CPA. Below is a summary of the attack performance on the SM4 implementation at 15 meters.

Method Type Traces to Recovery (15m) Success Rate
CPA Statistical > 50,000 10%
Classic CNN Deep Learning ~ 5,000 85%
Switch-T (Ours) Transformer ~ 1,500 99%
DAR-NN Domain Adaptation ~ 2,000 95%
